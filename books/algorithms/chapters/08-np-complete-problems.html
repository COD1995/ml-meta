      Search Problems

      Over the past seven chapters we have developed algorithms for finding shortest paths and minimum spanning trees in graphs, matchings in bipartite graphs, maximum increasing sub- sequences, maximum flows in networks, and so on. All these algorithms are efficient, because they are polynomial in the size of the input. However, in each case their time requirement grows as a polynomial function (such as n, n2 , or n3) of the size of the input.

      In the process of updating. 

      \begin{algorithm}
\caption{Generic Search Framework}
\begin{algorithmic}
\Procedure{Search}{$P_0$}
    \State $S \gets \{P_0\}$
    \While{$S \neq \emptyset$}
        \State choose $P$ from $S$, and remove it
        \State expand $P$ into $P_1, \dots, P_k$
        \For{$i = 1$ to $k$}
            \If{test($P_i$) succeeds}
                \State \textbf{return} solution
            \ElsIf{test($P_i$) fails}
                \State discard $P_i$
            \Else
                \State add $P_i$ to $S$
            \EndIf
        \EndFor
    \EndWhile
    \State \textbf{return} no solution
\EndProcedure
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
